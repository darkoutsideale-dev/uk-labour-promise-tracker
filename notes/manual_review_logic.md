# Promise Dataset and Manual Review Logic 
This work focused on structuring Labour's housing pledge dataset and defining manual review criteria for classifying each pledge.
This dataset consists of 18 housing related pledges extracted from Labour's 2024 manifesto. Each pledge was given a unique pledge ID, policy topic, keyword, evidence summary, review statis, and progress score.
Each pledge was classified according to a simple progress criterion. PLedge for which no relevant official evidence was found is marked as "not started" and 0 points were given. Pledgees with confirmed policy movements, parliamentary discussions, or government actions in progress are marked as "in progress" and 50 points are given. Pledgees with evidence of enacted laws or clear policy implementation are marked as "implemented" and 100 points are given.
A manual review was necessary because keyword matching alone cannot prove that certain pledges have actually been fulfilled. For example, some housing pledges can be found through congressional legislation, while others should be reviewed with long term evidence, such as housing supply statistics, budget documents, and local government updates.
